The neighbourhood

Testaccio: the Rome that Romans keep for themselves

Once the city's working river port and slaughterhouse district, this compact quarter has quietly become Rome's most authentic food-and-culture corner, without ever losing its everyday soul.

Mornings belong to the covered Mercato di Testaccio, where stalls sell just-picked produce, fresh pasta and the city's most famous panini. Evenings draw you to trattorias serving true Roman cooking (cacio e pepe, coda alla vaccinara, supplì) alongside craft-cocktail bars and the clubs carved into ancient Monte dei Cocci.

The Pyramid of Cestius, the leafy Non-Catholic Cemetery (Keats and Shelley rest here), Trastevere and the Colosseum are all within an easy stroll, yet you sleep among locals, not tourists.
